# EdTech Online Courses – Power BI Analysis

## Project Overview
This dashboard analyzes 2,800+ recorded courses across 18 categories and 52 sub-categories 
from a multi-platform EdTech dataset, covering ratings, viewer engagement, language availability, 
and instructor performance. The analysis identifies category-wise content and language trends, 
top-rated instructors, and the impact of subtitles and course duration on viewership — helping 
the startup prioritize what recorded lectures to launch next.

## Business Problem
An EdTech startup wanted to grow its recorded lecture offerings but needed data-driven insights 
to understand its existing course catalog, identify content gaps, and find growth opportunities 
across categories and languages.

## Questions Answered
1. Total number of courses in the dataset
2. Average course rating
3. Total and average views across all courses
4. Distribution of course types by category and sub-category
5. Average views by category, sub-category, and language
6. Distribution of languages across courses
7. Language preferences for the top 5 categories
8. Relationship between subtitle availability and views
9. Top 3 instructors per category and sub-category (by rating)
10. Relationship between course duration and views

## Key Findings
- English dominates the dataset at 97.37% of all courses, highlighting a major opportunity 
  to expand non-English content
- Data Science has the highest average registrations per course (6,326.70), ranking #1 among categories
- Course-type content drives the most total views (7.0M), while Specializations have the highest average views per course (3.4K)

## Conclusion
This analysis reveals that recorded lecture content is heavily concentrated in English (97%+ of 
courses) and a handful of top categories, with rating and engagement patterns varying notably by 
category, sub-category, and instructor. For the EdTech startup, the clearest growth opportunity 
lies in expanding underserved languages and high-engagement categories, while partnering with the 
consistently top-rated instructors identified in the analysis.
